The high court is a superior court of law that holds jurisdiction over a specific geographical area or a particular subject matter. It serves as the appellate court for cases that have been decided by lower courts and provides legal remedies and justice. The high court is composed of highly experienced judges who ensure the fair interpretation and application of the law.

The Supreme Court is the highest judicial authority in a country's legal system, serving as the final arbiter of legal disputes and constitutional matters. Its decisions are binding and have a far-reaching impact on legal precedents. Composed of esteemed justices, the Supreme Court plays a crucial role in upholding the rule of law and safeguarding the principles of justice and equality.

District courts are the trial courts of the federal judicial system, handling a wide range of civil and criminal cases within their designated districts. They are the first level of the federal court hierarchy, presided over by district judges who are responsible for conducting trials and rendering judgments. District courts play a vital role in ensuring access to justice and resolving disputes at the grassroots level of the federal legal system.
